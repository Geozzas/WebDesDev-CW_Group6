OUR GOALS

Ultimately, our goal is to raise awareness to protect, restore and promote sustainable use of our terrestrial ecosystems, sustainably manage forests and combat desertification. Additionally, our goal is to raise awareness to halt and reverse land degradation and biodiversity loss.

LIFE ON LAND

Terrestrial ecosystems, which encompass a wide range of environments including forests, grasslands, deserts, and tundra, are vital for sustaining human life, contributing to over half of global GDP and encompassing diverse cultural, spiritual, and economic values. Terrestrial ecosystems also play a crucial role in preserving the planet's well-being and sustaining its rich biodiversity, which encompasses a wide variety of organisms. Safeguarding and preserving these ecosystems is essential for fostering sustainable development and securing a habitable environment for generations to come.

CHALLENGES

However, the world faces a triple crisis of climate change, pollution and biodiversity loss, each of which presents significant challenges to both the environment and human societies.
Climate change: Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns.
Pollution: The major types of pollution are air pollution, water pollution, and land pollution. Pollution of all kinds can have negative effects on the environment and wildlife and often impacts human health and well-being.
Biodiversity Loss: Biodiversity, or the variety of all living things on our planet, has been declining at an alarming rate in recent years, mainly due to human activities, such as land use changes, pollution and climate change.

These escalating trends of forest loss, land degradation, and species extinction pose severe threats to both the planet and people. Forests, which play a crucial role in climate regulation, air purification, biodiversity conservation, and providing livelihoods for millions of people, are rapidly disappearing due to agriculture, urbanization, and resource extraction. Land degradation, caused by practices such as deforestation and soil erosion, undermines soil fertility, water quality, and food security. Biodiversity maintains the ecological balance and provides us with clean air, fresh water, good quality soil and crop pollination. It helps us fight climate change and adapt to it as well reduce the impact of natural hazards.

FUNDING

To halt and reverse biodiversity loss, a comprehensive strategy is necessary, which integrates both regulatory and voluntary measures, while also ensuring the mobilization and alignment of financing for biodiversity conservation efforts. Economic tools are pivotal in encouraging the preservation and sustainable utilization of biodiversity, and they can effectively mobilize funding while integrating biodiversity considerations across various sectors. These tools encompass policy measures like biodiversity-related taxes, fees, and charges, positive subsidies, payments for ecosystem services, and biodiversity offsets.

PROGRESS

Despite some progress in sustainable forest management, protected areas, and the uptake of national biodiversity values and natural capital accounting, most improvements have been modest. The recently adopted Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework outlines four outcome-oriented goals to be achieved by 2050 and 23 targets to be achieved by 2030.

A fundamental shift in humanity's relationship with nature is imperative to effectively address the interconnected crises of climate change, pollution, and biodiversity loss. Accelerated action is needed to tackle the root causes of these crises, which often stem from unsustainable human activities such as deforestation, fossil fuel consumption, industrial pollution, and overexploitation of natural resources. Furthermore, there is a pressing need for better recognition of the tremendous value of nature and the ecosystem services it provides. By acknowledging and appreciating the vital role of nature, we can foster greater appreciation for conservation efforts and promote more responsible stewardship of the planet's resources. Land and ecosystem restoration offer cost-effective solutions to address climate change, biodiversity loss, food and water security, and disaster impacts. To this end, governments, businesses and communities must collaborate to conserve natural areas, scale up nature-positive food production and develop green urban areas, infrastructure and supply chains.
